Agent & Portfolio Playbook

01

Regular vs Direct Plan Audit

Ask your agent to shift all regular plans to their direct equivalents on the same platform (e.g., MFCentral, Groww, Zerodha Coin). Regular plans carry a 0.5–1.5% higher expense ratio — that's money leaving your pocket every year silently.

02

ELSS Overlap Clean-Up

You currently hold both Samco ELSS and HDFC ELSS. Review the top-10 holdings of each. If there's >50% stock overlap, one of them is redundant. Diversification is only real if the funds hold different companies.

03

Monitor RankMF Rankings

RankMF.com gives a simple rank (1–10) for every fund. Ask your agent to red-flag any fund that drops below Rank 5 for two consecutive quarters — that's a signal to review, not panic-exit.

04

Annual Portfolio Review Meeting

Insist on a formal annual review meeting (not just a call). Your agent should bring the XIRR of each fund, not just absolute returns. XIRR adjusts for the timing of your SIPs — it's the honest number.

05

Goal-Tagging Each Fund

Every fund in your portfolio should be tagged to a goal (Retirement, Child Education, Emergency Corpus). If your agent can't tell you which fund serves which goal, the portfolio lacks strategy.

06

Rebalancing Trigger

Ask your agent to set a rebalancing rule: if any asset class deviates by >10% from the target allocation, trigger a review. This is discipline — not market timing.

ICICI Prudential Large Cap — The Naren Factor

S. Naren is one of India's most respected fund managers, known for contrarian calls. When markets are overheated, Naren builds cash (defensive). When markets fall, he deploys cash aggressively. This "Anchor Strategy" reduces volatility. You benefit most by staying invested during his defensive phases — don't exit because returns temporarily plateau.

01

SIP on Market Dip Days

For ICICI Pru Large Cap, consider increasing your SIP amount (step-up) whenever Nifty 50 falls more than 5% in a month. This is the retail investor's version of Naren's strategy — buy more when others are fearful.

02

Track AUM Growth

Keep a quarterly eye on ICICI Pru Large Cap's AUM. If it crosses ₹30,000 Cr, a large-cap fund may struggle to generate alpha (too big to buy small/mid gems). Monitor for any style drift.

03

Motilal Oswal Focused — Conviction Play

This fund holds a concentrated portfolio (max 30 stocks). It can underperform for long stretches, then deliver sharply. Suitable only if your investment horizon is 7+ years. Don't judge it on 1-year returns.

04

SEBI mandates multicap funds to hold at least 25% each in Large, Mid, and Small cap. SBI Multicap gives you automatic exposure across the market cap spectrum. Good as a core holding. Check its small-cap allocation during bull runs — it tends to go heavy there.

05

📖 Investor Glossary

🐂

Bull Market

When the market is rising or expected to rise. Investors are optimistic. Like a bull charging upward with its horns. A Bull Run usually means stocks are consistently going up for weeks or months.

🐻

Bear Market

When the market falls 20% or more from its peak. Investors are pessimistic. Like a bear swiping downward with its paw. During a bear market, patience and SIP discipline are your best friends.

😱

VIX (Volatility Index)

India VIX measures how "nervous" or "calm" the market is. A high VIX (above 20) = fear & wild swings. A low VIX (below 15) = calm & stable markets. Think of it as the market's "heartbeat speed."

🔵

NIFTY 50 — What's in it?

50 largest companies on NSE (National Stock Exchange). Examples: Reliance Industries, TCS, HDFC Bank, Infosys, ICICI Bank, L&T, ITC, Bajaj Finance. It covers ~65% of NSE's total market capitalization. Sectors: Banking & Finance (~35%), IT (~14%), Energy (~12%), Consumer (~10%), and more.

🟠

SENSEX — What's in it?

30 oldest and most established companies on BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange). Launched in 1986 — India's oldest index. Companies: Reliance, TCS, HDFC Bank, Infosys, Hindustan Unilever, Wipro, Maruti Suzuki, Nestle India, Sun Pharma, Asian Paints, and 20 more blue-chips. A single-day 1,000-point Sensex move is roughly a 1.5% move.

💹

XIRR

Extended Internal Rate of Return — the real return on your SIP investments, adjusted for when you invested. Always judge your mutual funds by XIRR, not just "absolute returns." A 20% return on ₹1 lakh is very different from 20% on ₹10 lakh.

📉

Drawdown

How much a fund/index fell from its peak before recovering. A 30% drawdown means it went from ₹100 to ₹70. Good funds recover faster. Midcap & small cap funds have higher drawdowns — that's the price you pay for higher long-term returns.

🧾

ELSS (Tax Saving Funds)

Equity Linked Saving Scheme — mutual funds that give you a tax deduction under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 lakh/year). They have a 3-year lock-in but invest in equities for long-term growth. The lock-in is per SIP instalment.

📊

Expense Ratio

The annual fee a mutual fund charges you, expressed as a % of your invested amount. Direct plans have lower expense ratios (0.1–0.8%) vs Regular plans (0.5–1.8%). Over 20 years, even a 1% difference can mean lakhs of rupees lost to fees.

🎯

ESG Investing

Environmental, Social, and Governance — investing in companies that score well on these factors. ESG funds avoid heavy polluters, companies with poor labour practices, or weak governance. They may underperform during commodity booms but aim for sustainable long-term growth.
